01 / TL;DR

You can ban the app. You can't ban the loop.

When India banned PUBG Mobile outright in 2020 over data-privacy concerns, Krafton didn't rebuild a new game — it relaunched the identical core loop as Battlegrounds Mobile India (BGMI) in 2021 and it grew from there. In Q1 2026, the PUBG franchise crossed ₩1 trillion (~$680M) in quarterly revenue for the first time, with BGMI paying users up 17% year-over-year. A 100-player last-man-standing loop with a 20-30 minute match length turned out to be more durable than any single app, platform, or even a national ban.

02 / The Problem

How do you design a session-based game that people play three times a day for years?

Most mobile games face declining engagement within months. PUBG Mobile's design challenge — and its central achievement — was building a match structure and reward system that made "one more round" feel low-cost enough to repeat constantly (average 33-minute sessions, 3 times daily) while the meta-progression (Royal Pass, ranked tiers, cosmetics) kept players returning across years, not weeks.

03 / Context — only what matters

The engagement numbers behind the design

  • Players average 33 minutes per session, logging in 3 times daily — a cadence that mirrors a habit far more than a typical mobile game session pattern.
  • D1 retention sits at 51%, well above the 30-40% typical range for competitive mobile shooters — an unusually strong first-day hook for the genre.
  • China's censored variant, Game for Peace, generates 69% of all 30-day player spending — proof the core loop transfers across radically different content restrictions (blood replaced with gold coins, eliminated players "transported away") without losing its retention power.
  • BGIS 2026's LAN finals drew record viewership in India — the esports layer on top of the core loop keeps building its own separate engagement engine years after the base game's initial launch.

04 / The Lens

Separating the loop's "must-have" mechanics from its "delighter" layers

01

Basic expectation: low-cost repeatability

A 20-30 minute match with clear win/loss resolution is table-stakes for the genre — without this, none of the retention mechanics below would have anything to attach to.

02

Performance factor: the Royal Pass / battle pass

Directly ties spend and time investment to visible progress — the more you engage, the more "sunk" the seasonal pass feels, a linear satisfaction driver rather than a delighter.

03

Delighter: culturally-specific collaborations

BGMI's anime and IPL-team collaborations, or the India-specific voice packs with local creators, aren't expected by players — they're what turns an ordinary session into a shareable, socially-visible moment, and they're the layer most responsible for BGMI's India-specific record revenue events.

05 / The AI Angle

Krafton is using AI to extend the loop itself, not just personalize marketing around it

Krafton's "AI for Game" initiative includes a Co-Playable Character (PUBG Ally) launching in beta — an AI teammate designed to change match dynamics directly, not just recommend content or optimize notifications. This is a materially different bet than most live-service games make with AI: instead of using AI purely for retention prediction or personalized offers, Krafton is using it to extend what the core 100-player loop can actually do, which is a much higher-risk, higher-reward product move.

06 / My Call

Protect the core loop's simplicity; let AI extend the delighter layer, not replace the basics

If I were leading product for the PUBG franchise, I would be cautious about the AI Co-Playable Character changing core match dynamics too aggressively — the 51% D1 retention exists because the loop is legible and fair, and adding AI teammates risks making outcomes feel less skill-determined, which is the fastest way to erode a competitive game's trust with its most loyal players.

The tradeoff I'd accept: slower AI feature rollout in the core game mode, in exchange for testing genuinely novel AI mechanics in a separate arcade/casual mode first — protecting the flagship loop's integrity while still learning what AI can add.

Developer Perspective: The less-discussed part of this story is the backend rebuild: relaunching as a India-specific app after a ban likely meant standing up separate data residency and compliance infrastructure, and maintaining that alongside the global PUBG codebase — a real technical-debt cost of operating under two regulatory regimes, not just a rebrand.

07 / How I'd Measure It

North Star + two supporting signals

★ North Star
D1/D7/D30 retention curve — the clearest signal of whether the core loop, not any single feature, is still driving habit formation
Signal 01

Signal 02
Perceived-fairness sentiment in new AI-mode betas — an early warning system before AI features reach the core competitive mode
