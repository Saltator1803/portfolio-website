01 / TL;DR

The quiet company just became the biggest one.

In April 2026, Anthropic overtook OpenAI in annualized revenue — $47B versus roughly $25B — despite having a fraction of OpenAI's consumer users (245M vs. 1.1B monthly users). A month later its $965B valuation passed OpenAI's $852B. The reason isn't a better chatbot. It's that Anthropic generates ~$192 in annual revenue per monthly active user versus OpenAI's ~$23 — an 8x difference — because it built for the customer who pays the most per seat: the enterprise.

02 / The Problem

How do you win a race everyone thinks is about consumer scale?

By 2024, the AI market narrative was entirely about consumer adoption — weekly active users, app store rankings, viral moments. Anthropic had none of ChatGPT's brand recognition and none of Google's built-in distribution through Search or Android. The strategic question was whether a smaller, less-known lab could win by optimizing for a completely different metric: revenue per user and depth of enterprise integration, rather than headline user counts that make for good press releases but not necessarily good businesses.

03 / Context — only what matters

The numbers behind the bet

  • Claude Code, launched as a standalone product in Feb 2025, hit $1B run-rate revenue by November 2025 — faster than any enterprise software product in history — and crossed $2.5B run-rate by February 2026.
  • Anthropic derives ~85% of revenue from business customers, the inverse of OpenAI's ~85% consumer-subscription mix.
  • 1 in 5 businesses on Ramp's payments platform now pay for Anthropic, up from 1 in 25 a year earlier — a 500% jump in enterprise adoption in twelve months.
  • 79% of OpenAI's enterprise customers also pay for Anthropic — proof the enterprise AI market rewards a multi-vendor strategy rather than one winner-take-all platform.

04 / The Lens

Three deliberate inversions of the "obvious" AI playbook

01

Distribution: multi-cloud instead of single-platform

Claude is the only frontier model available on all three major clouds — AWS Bedrock, Google Vertex AI, and Azure. Instead of picking one distribution partner, Anthropic made itself impossible for any single cloud vendor to gatekeep.

02

Product wedge: coding, not chat

Claude Code became the entry point for enterprise adoption — a specific, high-value job (writing and reviewing code) rather than a general-purpose assistant competing head-on with ChatGPT's brand recognition.

03

Land-and-expand, not land-and-monetize-everyone

Businesses starting with one use case — API, Claude Code, or Claude for Work — expand integrations organically. Customers spending $1M+ annually grew from a dozen to 500+ in two years by depth of integration, not volume of sign-ups.

05 / The AI Angle

Safety as a GTM asset, not a compliance cost

Anthropic's Public Benefit Corporation structure and safety-first branding are usually framed as an ethical stance — but they're also a genuine product differentiator with regulated enterprises (healthcare, finance, government) who need auditability and predictability more than raw capability. Claude for Enterprise now serves HIPAA-governed healthcare organizations, and government deployments (Maryland's benefits system, the Government of Alberta) suggest safety-as-positioning opens categories that a "move fast" consumer brand can't easily enter.

06 / My Call

Don't chase consumer share. Go deeper into regulated verticals.

If I were leading enterprise strategy at Anthropic, I would resist the temptation to fight for consumer mindshare against ChatGPT's 1.1B users — that's a battle fought on brand recognition, where Anthropic will always be second. Instead, double down on vertical-specific compliance tooling (healthcare, government, financial services) where "boring, reliable, auditable" beats "exciting" every time a procurement committee is involved.

The tradeoff I'd accept: slower top-line consumer growth in exchange for revenue that is stickier, higher-margin, and much harder for a competitor to dislodge once it's embedded in a regulated workflow.

07 / How I'd Measure It

North Star + two supporting signals

★ North Star
Revenue per monthly active user — the metric that actually explains Anthropic's valuation, not headline user count
Signal 01
% of customers spending $1M+/year — depth of enterprise lock-in over time
Signal 02
Multi-product attach rate (API + Claude Code + Claude for Work) per account — proxy for land-and-expand health

Anthropic didn't win by having more users than OpenAI — it won by making each user worth 8 times more.
