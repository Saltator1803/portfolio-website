01 / TL;DR

The vision wasn't wrong. The execution model was.

Krutrim launched in December 2023 promising a full-stack Indian AI ecosystem — foundation models, custom chips, cloud, and a consumer assistant. By 2026, it had paused its LLM and semiconductor initiatives, killed its consumer chatbot Kruti, lost at least six senior executives since April 2025, and shrunk from 550+ employees to roughly 150-160. It was also conspicuously absent from India's flagship AI summit in early 2026 — the exact stage a "national AI champion" should have owned. The company's own FY25 filings show ₹101.7 crore in revenue, with 90% coming from two Ola group entities rather than independent customers.

02 / The Problem

Can the operating instincts that built a ride-hailing company build a foundation-model lab?

Bhavish Aggarwal's track record — Ola Cabs, Ola Electric — was built on speed: move fast, launch ambitiously, iterate publicly. That playbook works when the constraint is operational execution in a market you already understand. AI research is a different kind of business: it rewards patient, deep technical investment and can't be willed into existence through announcement velocity. The strategic question Krutrim never resolved was whether its founder's greatest strength was actually a liability in this specific business.

03 / Context — only what matters

The pattern, in Krutrim's own numbers

  • Krutrim's consumer AI app recorded just over 100,000 downloads in its first six months — compared to ChatGPT's 110M+ users in India and DeepSeek's 40M+, in the same market, around the same period.
  • Nearly 200 employees were laid off across 2025 in multiple rounds, concentrated in the linguistics team — the group specifically responsible for building India-specific language capability, the company's stated core differentiator.
  • Competitors Sarvam AI, Gnani.ai, and the IIT-Bombay-led BharatGen consortium grew team strength and secured India AI Mission backing over the same period Krutrim was contracting.
  • Krutrim's five-member board has no independent directors — governance concerns were raised specifically because the company is substantially funded by, and sells services back to, its own sister companies.

04 / The Lens

Three compounding failures, not one

01

Scope failure: trying to build the entire stack at once

LLMs, chips, cloud, and a consumer assistant simultaneously meant no single bet got enough sustained resourcing to reach genuine competitiveness before attention moved to the next announcement.

02

Talent failure: senior hires brought in on a mission, managed like a sprint

Executives who left comfortable roles at Intel specifically to join a "national AI mission" left within about a year — a pattern industry insiders describe as chronic across Aggarwal's ventures, not unique to Krutrim.

03

Market failure: no external validation loop

With 90% of revenue coming from sister companies, Krutrim never had to prove product-market fit against real, price-sensitive, switching-capable customers — so its own metrics couldn't tell it when something wasn't working.

05 / The AI Angle

Foundation models don't reward first-mover announcements — they reward sustained compute and talent retention

Krutrim's pivot away from LLMs and chips toward being an "AI cloud infrastructure" reseller is a tacit admission that frontier model development requires a capital and talent commitment closer to Anthropic's or NVIDIA's than to a startup running on internal group revenue. The lesson for any AI PM: a sovereign-AI or national-champion narrative is not, by itself, a product strategy — it still has to answer who the paying, non-captive customer is and why they'd choose you over Sarvam, DeepSeek, or an API call to a frontier lab.

06 / My Call

Narrow the bet to one defensible layer, and retain the people who came for the mission

If I were advising Krutrim in 2024, I would have pushed to abandon the full-stack ambition immediately and concentrate every rupee and every senior hire on one layer — most plausibly Indic-language fine-tuning, where genuine data and cultural advantage exists, rather than chips or a general consumer chatbot competing directly with ChatGPT and DeepSeek on their own terms.

The tradeoff I'd accept: a much smaller, less headline-grabbing company in year one, in exchange for actually retaining the senior technical talent and reaching genuine product-market fit in a category Krutrim could plausibly defend.

07 / How I'd Measure It

North Star + two supporting signals

★ North Star
Revenue from non-Ola-group customers — the real test of product-market fit
Signal 01
Senior technical staff retention past 18 months — leading indicator of whether the mission narrative is holding
Signal 02
Independent developer adoption of the Indic-language model — proxy for whether the one chosen bet is actually winning

Krutrim didn't fail because India can't build AI — it failed because announcing an entire stack is not the same as building one layer of it well.
